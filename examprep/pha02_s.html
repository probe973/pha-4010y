Pharmacy Questions 2 - Solutions

Instructions

Below are the worked solutions for each question.

  1. What is 0.007 grams expressed in micrograms?
    To go from grams to milligrams, multiply by 1000: 0.007 g × 1000 = 7 mg To go from milligrams to micrograms, multiply by 1000: 7 mg × 1000 = 7,000 mcg
    Solution: 7,000 mcg
  2. What is 0.0015 kilometres expressed in millimetres?
    To go from kilometres to metres, multiply by 1000: 0.0015 km × 1000 = 1.5 m To go from metres to millimetres, multiply by 1000: 1.5 m × 1000 = 1,500 mm
    Solution: 1,500 mm
  3. What is 1 in 250 expressed as mg/mL?
    "1 in 250" means 1 gram in 250 mL. Convert grams to milligrams: 1 g = 1000 mg. So, 1000 mg in 250 mL. To find mg per mL, divide milligrams by millilitres: 1000 mg ÷ 250 mL = 4 mg/mL
    Solution: 4 mg/mL
  4. What is 6 mg/mL expressed as %w/v?
    %w/v means grams per 100 mL. We have 6 mg per 1 mL. To find mg per 100 mL: 6 mg/mL × 100 = 600 mg/100 mL. Convert milligrams to grams: 600 mg ÷ 1000 mg/g = 0.6 g. So, 0.6 g in 100 mL, which is 0.6% w/v.
    Solution: 0.6% w/v
  5. If a mixture is described as 8mg/ml, how many grams are required to make 150 millilitres of the mixture?
    First, find total milligrams: 8 mg/mL × 150 mL = 1200 mg Convert milligrams to grams: 1200 mg ÷ 1000 mg/g = 1.2 g
    Solution: 1.2 g
  6. If a drug is described as 1 part in 400, how many litres are required to provide a dose of 20g?
    "1 part in 400" means 1 gram in 400 mL. We need 20 grams. This is 20 times the amount. So, 20 g will be in 20 × 400 mL = 8000 mL. Convert millilitres to litres: 8000 mL ÷ 1000 mL/L = 8 L
    Solution: 8 L
  7. How many millilitres of a 4%w/v solution would be required to provide a dose of 120mg?
    Convert %w/v to mg/mL: 4% w/v = 4 grams in 100 mL = 4000 mg in 100 mL. So, 4000 mg ÷ 100 mL = 40 mg/mL. Now, find the volume needed for 120 mg: Volume = Total dose ÷ Concentration Volume = 120 mg ÷ 40 mg/mL = 3 mL
    Solution: 3 ml
  8. How much KCl in grams is required to make 300 millimoles, given that the atomic weight of potassium = 39 and chlorine = 35.5?
    Calculate the molecular weight of KCl: K (39) + Cl (35.5) = 74.5 g/mol. This means 1 mole of KCl weighs 74.5 grams. 1000 millimoles = 74.5 grams. We need 300 millimoles. 300 millimoles is 300/1000 = 0.3 moles. So, 0.3 × 74.5 g = 22.35 g
    Solution: 22.35 g
  9. How many grams of Magnesium Sulfate (molecular weight = 120) are required to produce 250 millilitres of a 3 molar solution?
    1 molar (1M) solution means 120 g in 1000 mL. 3 molar (3M) solution means 3 × 120 g in 1000 mL = 360 g in 1000 mL. We need to make 250 mL, which is one-quarter (1/4) of 1000 mL. So, we need one-quarter of 360 g: 360 g ÷ 4 = 90 g
    Solution: 90 g
  10. You need to make 600mL of a 3M solution from a 12M stock solution, how many millilitres of stock solution do you require?
    Use the formula C1V1 = C2V2: (12 M) × V1 = (3 M) × (600 mL) 12 × V1 = 1800 V1 = 1800 ÷ 12 V1 = 150 mL
    Solution: 150 ml
  11. If a person is 5 feet and 6 inches tall, what is this in metres (Assuming 1 inch = 2.5 cm)?
    Convert feet to inches: 5 feet × 12 inches/foot = 60 inches. Total height in inches = 60 inches + 6 inches = 66 inches. Convert inches to centimetres: 66 inches × 2.5 cm/inch = 165 cm. (Calculation: 66 × 2 = 132; 66 × 0.5 = 33; 132 + 33 = 165) Convert centimetres to metres: 165 cm ÷ 100 cm/m = 1.65 metres
    Solution: 1.65 metres
  12. If a patient is 9 stone 6 lbs, how much do they weigh in Kg (Assuming 2 lbs = 1 Kg)?
    Convert stone to pounds: 9 stone × 14 lbs/stone = 126 lbs. Total weight in pounds = 126 lbs + 6 lbs = 132 lbs. Convert pounds to kilograms: 132 lbs ÷ 2 lbs/Kg = 66 Kg
    Solution: 66 Kg
  13. What is the body surface area of someone who is 1.62 metres tall and 72 Kg in weight?
    (For Your Information: √2.89 = 1.7, √3.00 = 1.73, √3.24 = 1.8, √3.61 = 1.9)
    Formula: BSA = √(Ht (cm) x Weight (kg) / 3600)
    Convert height to cm: 1.62 metres = 162 cm. BSA = √((162 cm × 72 Kg) ÷ 3600) Simplify the fraction first: (162 × 72) ÷ 3600 Divide numerator and denominator by 36: 162 × (72 ÷ 36) = 162 × 2 = 324 3600 ÷ 36 = 100 So, 324 ÷ 100 = 3.24 BSA = √3.24 From the given list, √3.24 = 1.8.
    Solution: 1.8 m²
  14. What is 30 °C in °F?
    Use the formula F = (C × 9/5) + 32: F = (30 × 9/5) + 32 Simplify the multiplication: (30 ÷ 5) × 9 = 6 × 9 = 54. F = 54 + 32 F = 86 °F
    Solution: 86 °F
  15. How many millilitres of a 1 in 10 solution is required to produce 400mL of a 1 in 2,000 solution?
    Use the formula C1V1 = C2V2. C1 = 1/10 V1 = ? C2 = 1/2000 V2 = 400 mL (1/10) × V1 = (1/2000) × 400 To isolate V1, multiply both sides by the reciprocal of C1 (which is 10/1): V1 = (1/2000) × 400 × (10/1) V1 = (400 × 10) / 2000 V1 = 4000 / 2000 V1 = 2 mL
    Solution: 2 ml
  16. If a 70-year-old female has an ideal body weight of 60kg and an actual body weight of 65kg with a serum creatinine of 120 micromol/litre, what is their CrCl?
    Formula: CrCl (ml/min) = (140-Age) x Weight (Kg) / Serum creatinine (micromol/L)
    CrCl (ml/min) = (140 - 70) × 60 ÷ 120 CrCl (ml/min) = 70 × 60 ÷ 120 CrCl (ml/min) = 4200 ÷ 120 CrCl (ml/min) = 420 ÷ 12 CrCl (ml/min) = 35 ml/min
    Solution: 35 ml/min
  17. If Betamethasone cream is described as 2% w/w, in milligrams, how much active ingredient will be required to make 400 grams?
    2% w/w means 2 grams of active ingredient in 100 grams of cream. We need to make 400 grams of cream, which is 4 times 100 grams. So, we need 4 × 2 grams = 8 grams of active ingredient. Convert grams to milligrams: 8 g × 1000 mg/g = 8,000 mg
    Solution: 8,000 mg
  18. A 70 Kg patient requires 5 mg/Kg/minute for 10 minutes. This is to be delivered from a 2% w/v solution, how much solution will they need in ml?
    First, calculate the total amount of drug needed: 5 mg/Kg/minute × 70 Kg × 10 minutes = 3500 mg. Next, understand the concentration of the stock solution: 2% w/v = 2 grams in 100 mL = 2000 mg in 100 mL. Now, find the volume of solution needed: Volume = (Total drug needed / Concentration in mg) × Volume for concentration Volume = (3500 mg / 2000 mg) × 100 mL Simplify the fraction 3500/2000: 3500/2000 = 35/20 (cancel zeros) = 7/4 (divide by 5) Volume = (7/4) × 100 mL Volume = 1.75 × 100 mL = 175 mL
    Solution: 175 ml
  19. If a patient requires 5 mcg/minute of a drug for 8 hours, what is the total amount of drug they will receive in mg? Answer to 1 decimal place.
    First, convert hours to minutes: 8 hours × 60 minutes/hour = 480 minutes. Calculate total micrograms: 5 mcg/minute × 480 minutes = 2400 mcg. Convert micrograms to milligrams: 2400 mcg ÷ 1000 mcg/mg = 2.4 mg.
    Solution: 2.4 mg
  20. If the formula for Dextrose Solution is:
    • Dextrose: 250mg
    • Sodium Citrate: 30mg
    • Purified Water: to 5 mL
    How much Dextrose in milligrams is required to produce 200ml of the Dextrose Solution?
    The formula is for a total volume of 5 mL. We need to produce 200 mL. Calculate the scaling factor: 200 mL ÷ 5 mL = 40. Multiply the Dextrose quantity by the scaling factor: 250 mg × 40 = 10,000 mg
    Solution: 10,000 mg
