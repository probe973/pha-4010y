Pharmacy Questions 4 - Solutions

Instructions

Below are the worked solutions for each question.

  1. What is 750 milligrams expressed in grams?
    To go from milligrams to grams, divide by 1000: 750 mg ÷ 1000 = 0.75 g
    Solution: 0.75 g
  2. What is 0.8 litres expressed in millilitres?
    To go from litres to millilitres, multiply by 1000: 0.8 L × 1000 = 800 mL
    Solution: 800 mL
  3. What is 1 in 100 expressed as mg/mL?
    "1 in 100" means 1 gram in 100 mL. Convert grams to milligrams: 1 g = 1000 mg. So, 1000 mg in 100 mL. To find mg per mL, divide milligrams by millilitres: 1000 mg ÷ 100 mL = 10 mg/mL
    Solution: 10 mg/mL
  4. What is 2.5 mg/mL expressed as %w/v?
    %w/v means grams per 100 mL. We have 2.5 mg per 1 mL. To find mg per 100 mL: 2.5 mg/mL × 100 = 250 mg/100 mL. Convert milligrams to grams: 250 mg ÷ 1000 mg/g = 0.25 g. So, 0.25 g in 100 mL, which is 0.25% w/v.
    Solution: 0.25% w/v
  5. If a mixture is described as 12mg/ml, how many grams are required to make 100 millilitres of the mixture?
    First, find total milligrams: 12 mg/mL × 100 mL = 1200 mg Convert milligrams to grams: 1200 mg ÷ 1000 mg/g = 1.2 g
    Solution: 1.2 g
  6. If a drug is described as 1 part in 1000, how many litres are required to provide a dose of 25g?
    "1 part in 1000" means 1 gram in 1000 mL. We need 25 grams. This is 25 times the amount. So, 25 g will be in 25 × 1000 mL = 25000 mL. Convert millilitres to litres: 25000 mL ÷ 1000 mL/L = 25 L
    Solution: 25 L
  7. How many millilitres of a 1%w/v solution would be required to provide a dose of 50mg?
    Convert %w/v to mg/mL: 1% w/v = 1 gram in 100 mL = 1000 mg in 100 mL. So, 1000 mg ÷ 100 mL = 10 mg/mL. Now, find the volume needed for 50 mg: Volume = Total dose ÷ Concentration Volume = 50 mg ÷ 10 mg/mL = 5 mL
    Solution: 5 ml
  8. How much Sodium Bicarbonate in grams is required to make 500 millimoles, given that the molecular weight of Sodium Bicarbonate = 84?
    1 mole of Sodium Bicarbonate weighs 84 grams. 1000 millimoles = 84 grams. We need 500 millimoles. 500 millimoles is 500/1000 = 0.5 moles. So, 0.5 × 84 g = 42 g
    Solution: 42 g
  9. How many grams of Potassium Chloride (molecular weight = 74.5) are required to produce 100 millilitres of a 1 molar solution?
    1 molar (1M) solution means 74.5 g in 1000 mL. We need to make 100 mL, which is one-tenth (1/10) of 1000 mL. So, we need one-tenth of 74.5 g: 74.5 g ÷ 10 = 7.45 g
    Solution: 7.45 g
  10. You need to make 300mL of a 5M solution from a 15M stock solution, how many millilitres of stock solution do you require?
    Use the formula C1V1 = C2V2: (15 M) × V1 = (5 M) × (300 mL) 15 × V1 = 1500 V1 = 1500 ÷ 15 V1 = 100 mL
    Solution: 100 ml
  11. If a person is 5 feet and 10 inches tall, what is this in meters (Assuming 1 inch = 2.5 cm)?
    Convert feet to inches: 5 feet × 12 inches/foot = 60 inches. Total height in inches = 60 inches + 10 inches = 70 inches. Convert inches to centimetres: 70 inches × 2.5 cm/inch = 175 cm. (Calculation: 70 × 2 = 140; 70 × 0.5 = 35; 140 + 35 = 175) Convert centimetres to metres: 175 cm ÷ 100 cm/m = 1.75 meters
    Solution: 1.75 meters
  12. If a patient is 11 stone 8 lbs, how much do they weigh in Kg (Assuming 2 lbs = 1 Kg)?
    Convert stone to pounds: 11 stone × 14 lbs/stone = 154 lbs. Total weight in pounds = 154 lbs + 8 lbs = 162 lbs. Convert pounds to kilograms: 162 lbs ÷ 2 lbs/Kg = 81 Kg
    Solution: 81 Kg
  13. What is the body surface area of someone who is 1.7 metres tall and 64.8 Kg in weight?
    (For Your Information: √3.06 = 1.75, √3.24 = 1.8, √3.42 = 1.85, √3.61 = 1.9)
    Formula: BSA = √(Ht (cm) x Weight (kg) / 3600)
    Convert height to cm: 1.7 metres = 170 cm. BSA = √((170 cm × 64.8 Kg) ÷ 3600) Simplify the fraction first: (170 × 64.8) ÷ 3600 (17 × 648) ÷ 3600 (cancel a zero) Divide 648 by 36: 648 ÷ 36 = 18 So, (17 × 18) ÷ 100 17 × 18 = 306 306 ÷ 100 = 3.06 BSA = √3.06 From the given list, √3.06 = 1.75.
    Solution: 1.75 m²
  14. What is 15 °C in °F?
    Use the formula F = (C × 9/5) + 32: F = (15 × 9/5) + 32 Simplify the multiplication: (15 ÷ 5) × 9 = 3 × 9 = 27. F = 27 + 32 F = 59 °F
    Solution: 59 °F
  15. How many millilitres of a 1 in 25 solution is required to produce 500mL of a 1 in 12,500 solution?
    Use the formula C1V1 = C2V2. C1 = 1/25 V1 = ? C2 = 1/12500 V2 = 500 mL (1/25) × V1 = (1/12500) × 500 To isolate V1, multiply both sides by the reciprocal of C1 (which is 25/1): V1 = (1/12500) × 500 × (25/1) V1 = (500 × 25) / 12500 V1 = 12500 / 12500 V1 = 1 mL
    Solution: 1 ml
  16. If a 50-year-old female has an ideal body weight of 55kg and a serum creatinine of 90 micromol/litre, what is their CrCl?
    Formula: CrCl (ml/min) = (140-Age) x Weight (Kg) / Serum creatinine (micromol/L)
    CrCl (ml/min) = (140 - 50) × 55 ÷ 90 CrCl (ml/min) = 90 × 55 ÷ 90 CrCl (ml/min) = 55 ml/min
    Solution: 55 ml/min
  17. If Calamine Lotion is described as 15% w/w, in milligrams, how much active ingredient will be required to make 100 grams?
    15% w/w means 15 grams of active ingredient in 100 grams of lotion. We need to make 100 grams of lotion. So, we need 15 grams of active ingredient. Convert grams to milligrams: 15 g × 1000 mg/g = 15,000 mg
    Solution: 15,000 mg
  18. A 60 Kg patient requires 8 mg/Kg/minute for 10 minutes. This is to be delivered from a 4% w/v solution, how much solution will they need in ml?
    First, calculate the total amount of drug needed: 8 mg/Kg/minute × 60 Kg × 10 minutes = 4800 mg. Next, understand the concentration of the stock solution: 4% w/v = 4 grams in 100 mL = 4000 mg in 100 mL. Now, find the volume of solution needed: Volume = (Total drug needed / Concentration in mg) × Volume for concentration Volume = (4800 mg / 4000 mg) × 100 mL Simplify the fraction 4800/4000: 4800/4000 = 48/40 (cancel zeros) = 6/5 (divide by 8) Volume = (6/5) × 100 mL Volume = 1.2 × 100 mL = 120 mL
    Solution: 120 ml
  19. If a patient requires 20 mcg/minute of a drug for 3 hours, what is the total amount of drug they will receive in mg? Answer to 1 decimal place.
    First, convert hours to minutes: 3 hours × 60 minutes/hour = 180 minutes. Calculate total micrograms: 20 mcg/minute × 180 minutes = 3600 mcg. Convert micrograms to milligrams: 3600 mcg ÷ 1000 mcg/mg = 3.6 mg.
    Solution: 3.6 mg
  20. If the formula for Sodium Chloride 0.9% Solution is:
    • Sodium Chloride: 900mg
    • Purified Water: to 100 mL
    How much Sodium Chloride in milligrams is required to produce 500ml of the Sodium Chloride 0.9% Solution?
    The formula is for a total volume of 100 mL. We need to produce 500 mL. Calculate the scaling factor: 500 mL ÷ 100 mL = 5. Multiply the Sodium Chloride quantity by the scaling factor: 900 mg × 5 = 4,500 mg
    Solution: 4,500 mg
