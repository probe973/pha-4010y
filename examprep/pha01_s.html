Pharmacy Questions 1 - Solutions

Instructions

Below are the worked solutions for each question.

  1. What is 0.045 grams expressed in micrograms?
    To go from grams to milligrams, multiply by 1000: 0.045 g × 1000 = 45 mg To go from milligrams to micrograms, multiply by 1000: 45 mg × 1000 = 45,000 mcg
    Solution: 45,000 mcg
  2. What is 0.0036 kilometres expressed in millimetres?
    To go from kilometres to metres, multiply by 1000: 0.0036 km × 1000 = 3.6 m To go from metres to millimetres, multiply by 1000: 3.6 m × 1000 = 3,600 mm
    Solution: 3,600 mm
  3. What is 1 in 200 expressed as mg/mL?
    "1 in 200" means 1 gram in 200 mL. Convert grams to milligrams: 1 g = 1000 mg. So, 1000 mg in 200 mL. To find mg per mL, divide milligrams by millilitres: 1000 mg ÷ 200 mL = 5 mg/mL
    Solution: 5 mg/mL
  4. What is 4 mg/mL expressed as %w/v?
    %w/v means grams per 100 mL. We have 4 mg per 1 mL. To find mg per 100 mL: 4 mg/mL × 100 = 400 mg/100 mL. Convert milligrams to grams: 400 mg ÷ 1000 mg/g = 0.4 g. So, 0.4 g in 100 mL, which is 0.4% w/v.
    Solution: 0.4% w/v
  5. If a mixture is described as 6mg/ml, how many grams are required to make 120 millilitres of the mixture? Please give your answer to 2 decimal points.
    First, find total milligrams: 6 mg/mL × 120 mL = 720 mg Convert milligrams to grams: 720 mg ÷ 1000 mg/g = 0.72 g
    Solution: 0.72 g
  6. If a drug is described as 1 part in 500, how many litres are required to provide a dose of 15g?
    "1 part in 500" means 1 gram in 500 mL. We need 15 grams. This is 15 times the amount. So, 15 g will be in 15 × 500 mL = 7500 mL. Convert millilitres to litres: 7500 mL ÷ 1000 mL/L = 7.5 L
    Solution: 7.5 L
  7. How many millilitres of a 2%w/v solution would be required to provide a dose of 80mg?
    Convert %w/v to mg/mL: 2% w/v = 2 grams in 100 mL = 2000 mg in 100 mL. So, 2000 mg ÷ 100 mL = 20 mg/mL. Now, find the volume needed for 80 mg: Volume = Total dose ÷ Concentration Volume = 80 mg ÷ 20 mg/mL = 4 mL
    Solution: 4 ml
  8. How much NaCl in grams is required to make 200 millimoles, given that the atomic weight of sodium = 23 and chlorine = 35.5?
    Calculate the molecular weight of NaCl: Na (23) + Cl (35.5) = 58.5 g/mol. This means 1 mole of NaCl weighs 58.5 grams. 1000 millimoles = 58.5 grams. We need 200 millimoles. 200 millimoles is 200/1000 = 0.2 moles. So, 0.2 × 58.5 g = 11.7 g
    Solution: 11.7 g
  9. How many grams of Lithium Carbonate (molecular weight = 74) are required to produce 500 millilitres of a 2 molar solution?
    1 molar (1M) solution means 74 g in 1000 mL. 2 molar (2M) solution means 2 × 74 g in 1000 mL = 148 g in 1000 mL. We need to make 500 mL, which is half of 1000 mL. So, we need half of 148 g: 148 g ÷ 2 = 74 g
    Solution: 74 g
  10. You need to make 400mL of a 2M solution from a 10M stock solution, how many millilitres of stock solution do you require?
    Use the formula C1V1 = C2V2: (10 M) × V1 = (2 M) × (400 mL) 10 × V1 = 800 V1 = 800 ÷ 10 V1 = 80 mL
    Solution: 80 ml
  11. If a person is 5 feet and 4 inches tall, what is this in metres (Assuming 1 inch = 2.5 cm)?
    Convert feet to inches: 5 feet × 12 inches/foot = 60 inches. Total height in inches = 60 inches + 4 inches = 64 inches. Convert inches to centimetres: 64 inches × 2.5 cm/inch = 160 cm. (Calculation: 64 × 2 = 128; 64 × 0.5 = 32; 128 + 32 = 160) Convert centimetres to metres: 160 cm ÷ 100 cm/m = 1.6 metres
    Solution: 1.6 metres
  12. If a patient is 8 stone 2 lbs, how much do they weigh in Kg (Assuming 2 lbs = 1 Kg)?
    Convert stone to pounds: 8 stone × 14 lbs/stone = 112 lbs. Total weight in pounds = 112 lbs + 2 lbs = 114 lbs. Convert pounds to kilograms: 114 lbs ÷ 2 lbs/Kg = 57 Kg
    Solution: 57 Kg
  13. What is the body surface area of someone who is 1.8 metres tall and 81 Kg in weight?
    (For Your Information: √3.65 = 1.91, √3.82 = 1.95, √4.05 = 2.01, √4.12 = 2.03)
    Formula: BSA = √(Ht (cm) x Weight (kg) / 3600)
    Convert height to cm: 1.8 metres = 180 cm. BSA = √((180 cm × 81 Kg) ÷ 3600) Simplify the fraction first: (180 × 81) ÷ 3600 Divide numerator and denominator by 180 (since 3600 = 180 × 20): (81) ÷ 20 81 ÷ 20 = 4.05 BSA = √4.05 From the given list, √4.05 = 2.01.
    Solution: 2.01 m²
  14. What is 40 °C in °F?
    Use the formula F = (C × 9/5) + 32: F = (40 × 9/5) + 32 Simplify the multiplication: (40 ÷ 5) × 9 = 8 × 9 = 72. F = 72 + 32 F = 104 °F
    Solution: 104 °F
  15. How many millilitres of a 1 in 20 solution is required to produce 250mL of a 1 in 5,000 solution?
    Use the formula C1V1 = C2V2. C1 = 1/20 V1 = ? C2 = 1/5000 V2 = 250 mL (1/20) × V1 = (1/5000) × 250 To isolate V1, multiply both sides by the reciprocal of C1 (which is 20/1): V1 = (1/5000) × 250 × (20/1) V1 = (250 × 20) / 5000 V1 = 5000 / 5000 V1 = 1 mL
    Solution: 1 ml
  16. If a 60-year-old male has an ideal body weight of 70kg and an actual body weight of 75kg with a serum creatinine of 100 micromol/litre, what is their CrCl?
    Formula: CrCl (ml/min) = (140-Age) x Weight (Kg) / Serum creatinine (micromol/L)
    CrCl (ml/min) = (140 - 60) × 70 ÷ 100 CrCl (ml/min) = 80 × 70 ÷ 100 CrCl (ml/min) = 5600 ÷ 100 CrCl (ml/min) = 56 ml/min
    Solution: 56 ml/min
  17. If Hydrocortisone cream is described as 5% w/w, in milligrams, how much active ingredient will be required to make 300 grams?
    5% w/w means 5 grams of active ingredient in 100 grams of cream. We need to make 300 grams of cream, which is 3 times 100 grams. So, we need 3 × 5 grams = 15 grams of active ingredient. Convert grams to milligrams: 15 g × 1000 mg/g = 15,000 mg
    Solution: 15,000 mg
  18. A 50 Kg patient requires 10 mg/Kg/minute for 15 minutes. This is to be delivered from a 5% w/v solution, how much solution will they need in ml?
    First, calculate the total amount of drug needed: 10 mg/Kg/minute × 50 Kg × 15 minutes = 7500 mg. Next, understand the concentration of the stock solution: 5% w/v = 5 grams in 100 mL = 5000 mg in 100 mL. Now, find the volume of solution needed: Volume = (Total drug needed / Concentration in mg) × Volume for concentration Volume = (7500 mg / 5000 mg) × 100 mL Simplify the fraction 7500/5000: 7500/5000 = 75/50 (cancel zeros) = 3/2 (divide by 25) Volume = (3/2) × 100 mL Volume = 1.5 × 100 mL = 150 mL
    Solution: 150 ml
  19. If a patient requires 10 mcg/minute of a drug for 6 hours, what is the total amount of drug they will receive in mg? Answer to 1 decimal place.
    First, convert hours to minutes: 6 hours × 60 minutes/hour = 360 minutes. Calculate total micrograms: 10 mcg/minute × 360 minutes = 3600 mcg. Convert micrograms to milligrams: 3600 mcg ÷ 1000 mcg/mg = 3.6 mg.
    Solution: 3.6 mg
  20. If the formula for Oral Rehydration Salts is:
    • Glucose: 100mg
    • Sodium Chloride: 50mg
    • Potassium Chloride: 20mg
    • Purified Water: to 10 mL
    How much Glucose in milligrams is required to produce 400ml of the Oral Rehydration Salts solution?
    The formula is for a total volume of 10 mL. We need to produce 400 mL. Calculate the scaling factor: 400 mL ÷ 10 mL = 40. Multiply the Glucose quantity by the scaling factor: 100 mg × 40 = 4,000 mg
    Solution: 4,000 mg
