  • A {{include.q.mass}} kg patient requires {{include.q.rate}} mg/kg/minute for {{include.q.time}} minutes, delivered by a {{include.q.conc}} %w/v solution. How much solution will they need in ml?
    Solution

    Step 1: Find rate per minute using patient mass:

    ${{include.q.rate}} \text{mg/kg/min} \times {{include.q.mass}} \text{kg} = {{ratetime}} \text{mg/min}$

    Step 2: Find total mg of drug

    ${{ratetime}} \text{mg/min} \times {{include.q.time}} \text{min} = {{need}} \text{mg}$

    Step 3: Make the concentration in the same units as required drug:

    ${{include.q.conc}} \text{% w/v} = {{include.q.conc}} \text{g/100 ml} = {{have}} \text{mg/100 ml}$

    Step 4: Calculate the volume in ml:

    $\frac{ {{need}} }{ {{have}} } \times 100 \text{ml} = {{volume}} \text{ml}$

    Solution: {{volume}} ml
