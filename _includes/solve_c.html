  • A {{include.q.mass | format: "%g"}} kg patient requires {{include.q.rate | format: "%g"}} mg/kg/minute for {{include.q.time | format: "%g"}} minutes, delivered by a {{include.q.conc | format: "%g"}} %w/v solution. How much solution will they need in ml?
    Solution

    Step 1: Find rate per minute using patient mass:

    ${{include.q.rate | format: "%g"}} \text{mg/kg/min} \times {{include.q.mass | format: "%g"}} \text{kg} = {{ratetime | format: "%g"}} \text{mg/min}$

    Step 2: Find total mg of drug

    ${{ratetime | format: "%g"}} \text{mg/min} \times {{include.q.time | format: "%g"}} \text{min} = {{need | format: "%g"}} \text{mg}$

    Step 3: Make the concentration in the same units as required drug:

    ${{include.q.conc | format: "%g"}} \text{% w/v} = {{include.q.conc | format: "%g"}} \text{g/100 ml} = {{have | format: "%g"}} \text{mg/100 ml}$

    Step 4: Calculate the volume in ml:

    $\frac{ {{need | format: "%g"}} }{ {{have | format: "%g"}} } \times 100 \text{ml} = {{volume | format: "%g"}} \text{ml}$

    Solution: {{volume | format: "%g"}} ml
