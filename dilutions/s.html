Dilution Principles Solutions

Instructions

Below are the worked solutions for each question.

For Your Information:

Formula: C1V1 = C2V2
  1. You need to prepare 500 mL of a 2% w/v solution. You have a stock solution of 10% w/v. How much of the stock solution do you need in mL?
    C1 = 10% w/v (stock concentration) V1 = ? (volume of stock needed) C2 = 2% w/v (desired concentration) V2 = 500 mL (desired final volume) Using C1V1 = C2V2: 10 × V1 = 2 × 500 10 × V1 = 1000 V1 = 1000 ÷ 10 V1 = 100 mL
    Solution: 100 mL
  2. You have 20 mL of a 50 mg/mL solution. You dilute it to a final concentration of 10 mg/mL. What is the final volume in mL?
    C1 = 50 mg/mL (initial concentration) V1 = 20 mL (initial volume) C2 = 10 mg/mL (desired final concentration) V2 = ? (final volume) Using C1V1 = C2V2: 50 × 20 = 10 × V2 1000 = 10 × V2 V2 = 1000 ÷ 10 V2 = 100 mL
    Solution: 100 mL
  3. If you dilute 200 mL of a 10% w/v solution to a final volume of 400 mL, what is the final concentration in %w/v?
    C1 = 10% w/v (initial concentration) V1 = 200 mL (initial volume) C2 = ? (final concentration) V2 = 400 mL (final volume) Using C1V1 = C2V2: 10 × 200 = C2 × 400 2000 = C2 × 400 C2 = 2000 ÷ 400 C2 = 5% w/v
    Solution: 5% w/v
  4. A 50 mL stock solution is diluted to a final volume of 250 mL, resulting in a concentration of 20 mg/mL. What was the original concentration of the stock solution in mg/mL?
    C1 = ? (original concentration) V1 = 50 mL (initial volume) C2 = 20 mg/mL (final concentration) V2 = 250 mL (final volume) Using C1V1 = C2V2: C1 × 50 = 20 × 250 C1 × 50 = 5000 C1 = 5000 ÷ 50 C1 = 100 mg/mL
    Solution: 100 mg/mL
  5. You dilute 50 mL of a 4% w/v solution to a final volume of 200 mL. What is the final concentration in mg/mL?
    First, convert initial concentration to mg/mL: 4% w/v = 4 g in 100 mL = 4000 mg in 100 mL = 40 mg/mL. C1 = 40 mg/mL (initial concentration) V1 = 50 mL (initial volume) C2 = ? (final concentration) V2 = 200 mL (final volume) Using C1V1 = C2V2: 40 × 50 = C2 × 200 2000 = C2 × 200 C2 = 2000 ÷ 200 C2 = 10 mg/mL
    Solution: 10 mg/mL
  6. You dilute 100 mL of a 20 mg/mL solution to a final volume of 500 mL. What is the final concentration in %w/v?
    C1 = 20 mg/mL (initial concentration) V1 = 100 mL (initial volume) C2 = ? (final concentration in mg/mL first) V2 = 500 mL (final volume) Using C1V1 = C2V2: 20 × 100 = C2 × 500 2000 = C2 × 500 C2 = 2000 ÷ 500 C2 = 4 mg/mL Now, convert 4 mg/mL to %w/v: 4 mg/mL = 4 mg in 1 mL = 400 mg in 100 mL = 0.4 g in 100 mL = 0.4% w/v
    Solution: 0.4% w/v
  7. You dilute 100 mL of a 1 in 100 solution to a final volume of 500 mL. What is the final strength expressed as 1 in X?
    Using C1V1 = C2V2: C1 = 1/100 (initial strength) V1 = 100 mL (initial volume) C2 = 1/X (final strength, where X is unknown) V2 = 500 mL (final volume) (1/100) × 100 = (1/X) × 500 1 = 500/X To solve for X, cross-multiply: 1 × X = 500 × 1 X = 500 So, the final strength is 1 in 500.
    Solution: 1 in 500
    Alternative Method (Dilution Factor): Dilution Factor = Final Volume ÷ Initial Volume Dilution Factor = 500 mL ÷ 100 mL = 5 Final Strength = Initial Strength × Dilution Factor (Note: For "1 in X" ratios, a dilution factor of 5 means the "X" value increases by a factor of 5) Final Strength = 1 in (100 × 5) Final Strength = 1 in 500
  8. You need to make 200 mL of a 1 in 2000 solution. You have a stock solution of 1 in 500. How much of the stock solution do you need in mL?
    Using C1V1 = C2V2: C1 = 1/500 (stock strength) V1 = ? (volume of stock needed) C2 = 1/2000 (desired strength) V2 = 200 mL (desired final volume) (1/500) × V1 = (1/2000) × 200 To isolate V1, multiply both sides by the reciprocal of C1 (which is 500/1): V1 = (1/2000) × 200 × (500/1) V1 = (1 × 200 × 500) / (2000 × 1) V1 = 100000 / 2000 V1 = 100 / 2 V1 = 50 mL
    Solution: 50 mL
    Alternative Method (Dilution Factor): Dilution Factor = Initial Strength ÷ Final Strength Dilution Factor = (1/500) ÷ (1/2000) Dilution Factor = (1/500) × (2000/1) Dilution Factor = 2000 ÷ 500 = 4 Volume of Stock Needed = Desired Final Volume ÷ Dilution Factor Volume of Stock Needed = 200 mL ÷ 4 Volume of Stock Needed = 50 mL
  9. You have a stock solution of 25% w/v. You need to prepare 100 mL of a 5% w/v solution. How much of the stock solution do you need in mL?
    C1 = 25% w/v V1 = ? C2 = 5% w/v V2 = 100 mL Using C1V1 = C2V2: 25 × V1 = 5 × 100 25 × V1 = 500 V1 = 500 ÷ 25 V1 = 20 mL
    Solution: 20 mL
  10. You have 500 mL of a 2 mg/mL solution. You want to dilute it to a final concentration of 0.5 mg/mL. What is the final volume in mL?
    C1 = 2 mg/mL V1 = 500 mL C2 = 0.5 mg/mL V2 = ? Using C1V1 = C2V2: 2 × 500 = 0.5 × V2 1000 = 0.5 × V2 To find V2, divide 1000 by 0.5 (which is the same as multiplying by 2): V2 = 1000 ÷ 0.5 V2 = 2000 mL
    Solution: 2000 mL
  11. You need to prepare 250 mL of a 4 mg/mL solution. You have a stock solution of 20 mg/mL. How much of the stock solution do you need in mL?

  12. If you dilute 50 mL of an 8% w/v solution to a final volume of 400 mL, what is the final concentration in %w/v?

    Solution: 1% w/v
  13. You have 15 mL of a 1 in 200 solution. You dilute it to a final strength of 1 in 1000. What is the final volume in mL?
    Using C1V1 = C2V2: C1 = 1/200 (initial strength) V1 = 15 mL (initial volume) C2 = 1/1000 (final strength) V2 = ? (final volume) (1/200) × 15 = (1/1000) × V2 To isolate V2, multiply both sides by the reciprocal of C2 (which is 1000/1): V2 = (1/200) × 15 × (1000/1) V2 = (1 × 15 × 1000) / (200 × 1) V2 = 15000 / 200 V2 = 150 / 2 V2 = 75 mL
    Solution: 75 mL
    Alternative Method (Dilution Factor): Dilution Factor = Initial Strength ÷ Final Strength Dilution Factor = (1/200) ÷ (1/1000) Dilution Factor = (1/200) × (1000/1) Dilution Factor = 1000 ÷ 200 = 5 Final Volume = Initial Volume × Dilution Factor Final Volume = 15 mL × 5 Final Volume = 75 mL

  17. You dilute 250 mL of a 1 in 500 solution to a final volume of 1000 mL. What is the final strength expressed as 1 in X?

    Solution: 1 in 2000
    Alternative Method (Dilution Factor): Dilution Factor = Final Volume ÷ Initial Volume Dilution Factor = 1000 mL ÷ 250 mL = 4 Final Strength = Initial Strength × Dilution Factor (Note: For "1 in X" ratios, a dilution factor of 4 means the "X" value increases by a factor of 4) Final Strength = 1 in (500 × 4) Final Strength = 1 in 2000
