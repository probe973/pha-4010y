Pharmacy Questions 4

Instructions

  • Answer ALL questions on this paper.
  • All questions have equal weighting.
  • All answers must include units, where appropriate. Answers without units (or incorrect units) will be marked as incorrect.
  • Calculators of any description are NOT allowed.
  1. What is 750 milligrams expressed in grams?
  2. What is 0.8 litres expressed in millilitres?
  3. What is 1 in 100 expressed as mg/mL?
  4. What is 2.5 mg/mL expressed as %w/v?
  5. If a mixture is described as 12mg/ml, how many grams are required to make 100 millilitres of the mixture?
  6. If a drug is described as 1 part in 1000, how many litres are required to provide a dose of 25g?
  7. How many millilitres of a 1%w/v solution would be required to provide a dose of 50mg?
  8. How much Sodium Bicarbonate in grams is required to make 500 millimoles, given that the molecular weight of Sodium Bicarbonate = 84?
  9. How many grams of Potassium Chloride (molecular weight = 74.5) are required to produce 100 millilitres of a 1 molar solution?
  10. You need to make 300mL of a 5M solution from a 15M stock solution, how many millilitres of stock solution do you require?
  11. If a person is 5 feet and 10 inches tall, what is this in meters (Assuming 1 inch = 2.5 cm)?
  12. If a patient is 11 stone 8 lbs, how much do they weigh in Kg (Assuming 2 lbs = 1 Kg)?
  13. What is the body surface area of someone who is 1.7 metres tall and 64.8 Kg in weight?
    (For Your Information: √3.06 = 1.75, √3.24 = 1.8, √3.42 = 1.85, √3.61 = 1.9)
    Formula: BSA = √(Ht (cm) x Weight (kg) / 3600)
  14. What is 15 °C in °F?
  15. How many millilitres of a 1 in 25 solution is required to produce 500mL of a 1 in 12,500 solution?
  16. If a 50-year-old female has an ideal body weight of 55kg and a serum creatinine of 90 micromol/litre, what is their CrCl?
    Formula: CrCl (ml/min) = (140-Age) x Weight (Kg) / Serum creatinine (micromol/L)
  17. If Calamine Lotion is described as 15% w/w, in milligrams, how much active ingredient will be required to make 100 grams?
  18. A 60 Kg patient requires 8 mg/Kg/minute for 10 minutes. This is to be delivered from a 4% w/v solution, how much solution will they need in ml?
  19. If a patient requires 20 mcg/minute of a drug for 3 hours, what is the total amount of drug they will receive in mg? Answer to 1 decimal place.
