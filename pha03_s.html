Pharmacy Questions 3 - Solutions

Instructions

Below are the worked solutions for each question.

  1. What is 0.005 grams expressed in micrograms?
    To go from grams to milligrams, multiply by 1000: 0.005 g × 1000 = 5 mg To go from milligrams to micrograms, multiply by 1000: 5 mg × 1000 = 5,000 mcg
    Solution: 5,000 mcg
  2. What is 0.0008 kilometres expressed in millimetres?
    To go from kilometres to metres, multiply by 1000: 0.0008 km × 1000 = 0.8 m To go from metres to millimetres, multiply by 1000: 0.8 m × 1000 = 800 mm
    Solution: 800 mm
  3. What is 1 in 500 expressed as mg/mL?
    "1 in 500" means 1 gram in 500 mL. Convert grams to milligrams: 1 g = 1000 mg. So, 1000 mg in 500 mL. To find mg per mL, divide milligrams by millilitres: 1000 mg ÷ 500 mL = 2 mg/mL
    Solution: 2 mg/mL
  4. What is 5 mg/mL expressed as %w/v?
    %w/v means grams per 100 mL. We have 5 mg per 1 mL. To find mg per 100 mL: 5 mg/mL × 100 = 500 mg/100 mL. Convert milligrams to grams: 500 mg ÷ 1000 mg/g = 0.5 g. So, 0.5 g in 100 mL, which is 0.5% w/v.
    Solution: 0.5% w/v
  5. If a mixture is described as 4mg/ml, how many grams are required to make 200 millilitres of the mixture?
    First, find total milligrams: 4 mg/mL × 200 mL = 800 mg Convert milligrams to grams: 800 mg ÷ 1000 mg/g = 0.8 g
    Solution: 0.8 g
  6. If a drug is described as 1 part in 200, how many litres are required to provide a dose of 10g?
    "1 part in 200" means 1 gram in 200 mL. We need 10 grams. This is 10 times the amount. So, 10 g will be in 10 × 200 mL = 2000 mL. Convert millilitres to litres: 2000 mL ÷ 1000 mL/L = 2 L
    Solution: 2 L
  7. How many millilitres of a 5%w/v solution would be required to provide a dose of 250mg?
    Convert %w/v to mg/mL: 5% w/v = 5 grams in 100 mL = 5000 mg in 100 mL. So, 5000 mg ÷ 100 mL = 50 mg/mL. Now, find the volume needed for 250 mg: Volume = Total dose ÷ Concentration Volume = 250 mg ÷ 50 mg/mL = 5 mL
    Solution: 5 ml
  8. How much Magnesium Sulfate in grams is required to make 400 millimoles, given that the molecular weight of Magnesium Sulfate = 120?
    1 mole of Magnesium Sulfate weighs 120 grams. 1000 millimoles = 120 grams. We need 400 millimoles. 400 millimoles is 400/1000 = 0.4 moles. So, 0.4 × 120 g = 48 g
    Solution: 48 g
  9. How many grams of Glucose (molecular weight = 180) are required to produce 250 millilitres of a 2 molar solution?
    1 molar (1M) solution means 180 g in 1000 mL. 2 molar (2M) solution means 2 × 180 g in 1000 mL = 360 g in 1000 mL. We need to make 250 mL, which is one-quarter (1/4) of 1000 mL. So, we need one-quarter of 360 g: 360 g ÷ 4 = 90 g
    Solution: 90 g
  10. You need to make 500mL of a 3M solution from a 15M stock solution, how many millilitres of stock solution do you require?
    Use the formula C1V1 = C2V2: (15 M) × V1 = (3 M) × (500 mL) 15 × V1 = 1500 V1 = 1500 ÷ 15 V1 = 100 mL
    Solution: 100 ml
  11. If a person is 5 feet and 2 inches tall, what is this in meters (Assuming 1 inch = 2.5 cm)?
    Convert feet to inches: 5 feet × 12 inches/foot = 60 inches. Total height in inches = 60 inches + 2 inches = 62 inches. Convert inches to centimetres: 62 inches × 2.5 cm/inch = 155 cm. (Calculation: 62 × 2 = 124; 62 × 0.5 = 31; 124 + 31 = 155) Convert centimetres to metres: 155 cm ÷ 100 cm/m = 1.55 meters
    Solution: 1.55 meters
  12. If a patient is 12 stone 4 lbs, how much do they weigh in Kg (Assuming 2 lbs = 1 Kg)?
    Convert stone to pounds: 12 stone × 14 lbs/stone = 168 lbs. Total weight in pounds = 168 lbs + 4 lbs = 172 lbs. Convert pounds to kilograms: 172 lbs ÷ 2 lbs/Kg = 86 Kg
    Solution: 86 Kg
  13. What is the body surface area of someone who is 1.6 metres tall and 81 Kg in weight?
    (For Your Information: √3.24 = 1.8, √3.61 = 1.9, √4.00 = 2.0)
    Formula: BSA = √(Ht (cm) x Weight (kg) / 3600)
    Convert height to cm: 1.6 metres = 160 cm. BSA = √((160 cm × 81 Kg) ÷ 3600) Simplify the fraction first: (160 × 81) ÷ 3600 Divide numerator and denominator by 40: (4 × 81) ÷ 90 324 ÷ 90 = 3.6 BSA = √3.6 From the given list, √3.6 = 1.9.
    Solution: 1.9 m²
  14. What is 25 °C in °F?
    Use the formula F = (C × 9/5) + 32: F = (25 × 9/5) + 32 Simplify the multiplication: (25 ÷ 5) × 9 = 5 × 9 = 45. F = 45 + 32 F = 77 °F
    Solution: 77 °F
  15. How many millilitres of a 1 in 50 solution is required to produce 200mL of a 1 in 10,000 solution?
    Use the formula C1V1 = C2V2. C1 = 1/50 V1 = ? C2 = 1/10000 V2 = 200 mL (1/50) × V1 = (1/10000) × 200 To isolate V1, multiply both sides by the reciprocal of C1 (which is 50/1): V1 = (1/10000) × 200 × (50/1) V1 = (200 × 50) / 10000 V1 = 10000 / 10000 V1 = 1 mL
    Solution: 1 ml
  16. If an 80-year-old male has an ideal body weight of 65kg and a serum creatinine of 130 micromol/litre, what is their CrCl?
    Formula: CrCl (ml/min) = (140-Age) x Weight (Kg) / Serum creatinine (micromol/L)
    CrCl (ml/min) = (140 - 80) × 65 ÷ 130 CrCl (ml/min) = 60 × 65 ÷ 130 CrCl (ml/min) = 3900 ÷ 130 CrCl (ml/min) = 390 ÷ 13 CrCl (ml/min) = 30 ml/min
    Solution: 30 ml/min
  17. If Salicylic Acid cream is described as 3% w/w, in milligrams, how much active ingredient will be required to make 200 grams?
    3% w/w means 3 grams of active ingredient in 100 grams of cream. We need to make 200 grams of cream, which is 2 times 100 grams. So, we need 2 × 3 grams = 6 grams of active ingredient. Convert grams to milligrams: 6 g × 1000 mg/g = 6,000 mg
    Solution: 6,000 mg
  18. A 40 Kg patient requires 20 mg/Kg/minute for 5 minutes. This is to be delivered from a 10% w/v solution, how much solution will they need in ml?
    First, calculate the total amount of drug needed: 20 mg/Kg/minute × 40 Kg × 5 minutes = 4000 mg. Next, understand the concentration of the stock solution: 10% w/v = 10 grams in 100 mL = 10,000 mg in 100 mL. Now, find the volume of solution needed: Volume = (Total drug needed / Concentration in mg) × Volume for concentration Volume = (4000 mg / 10000 mg) × 100 mL Simplify the fraction 4000/10000: 4000/10000 = 4/10 (cancel zeros) = 2/5 (divide by 2) Volume = (2/5) × 100 mL Volume = 0.4 × 100 mL = 40 mL
    Solution: 40 ml
  19. If a patient requires 15 mcg/minute of a drug for 4 hours, what is the total amount of drug they will receive in mg? Answer to 1 decimal place.
    First, convert hours to minutes: 4 hours × 60 minutes/hour = 240 minutes. Calculate total micrograms: 15 mcg/minute × 240 minutes = 3600 mcg. Convert micrograms to milligrams: 3600 mcg ÷ 1000 mcg/mg = 3.6 mg.
    Solution: 3.6 mg
  20. If the formula for Paracetamol Suspension is:
    • Paracetamol: 120mg
    • Vehicle: to 5 mL
    How much Paracetamol in milligrams is required to produce 250ml of the Paracetamol Suspension?
    The formula is for a total volume of 5 mL. We need to produce 250 mL. Calculate the scaling factor: 250 mL ÷ 5 mL = 50. Multiply the Paracetamol quantity by the scaling factor: 120 mg × 50 = 6,000 mg
    Solution: 6,000 mg
