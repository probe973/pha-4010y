                <details class="solution-details">

                    <div class="solution-content">

                        <p>$\rightarrow 1000~mg : 200~ml$</p>

        <h3>Dilutions</h3>
        If you have stock of a medication that is given as 1 in 100, and you need to create 40 ml of a 1 in 200 solution, how many ml do you need to take from the concentrate?
        <details class="solution-details">
            <summary>Solution</summary>
            <div class="solution-content">
                <p>Using the dilution formula $C1V1 = C2V2$</p>
                <p>$C1 = \frac{1}{100} = 0.01$ (stock concentration)</p>
                <p>$V1 = ?$ (volume of concentrate needed)</p>
                <p>$C2 = \frac{1}{200} = 0.005$ (desired final concentration)</p>
                <p>$V2 = 40~ml$ (desired final volume)</p>
                <p>$0.01 \times V1 = 0.005 \times 40$</p>
                <p>$V1 = \frac{0.005 \times 40}{0.01} = \frac{0.2}{0.01}$</p>
                <p>$V1 = \textbf{20 ml}$</p>
            </div>
        </details>
    </li>
