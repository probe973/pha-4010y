Pharmacy Questions 5 - Solutions

Instructions

Below are the worked solutions for each question.

  1. What is 2500 grams expressed in kilograms?
    To go from grams to kilograms, divide by 1000: 2500 g ÷ 1000 = 2.5 kg
    Solution: 2.5 kg
  2. What is 0.000005 kilograms expressed in micrograms?
    To go from kilograms to grams, multiply by 1000: 0.000005 kg × 1000 = 0.005 g To go from grams to milligrams, multiply by 1000: 0.005 g × 1000 = 5 mg To go from milligrams to micrograms, multiply by 1000: 5 mg × 1000 = 5,000 mcg
    Solution: 5,000 mcg
  3. What is 5 mg/mL expressed as 1 in X?
    5 mg/mL means 5 milligrams in 1 millilitre. We want to find out how many millilitres contain 1 gram. If 5 mg is in 1 mL, Then 5000 mg (which is 5 g) is in 1000 mL. To find how many mL for 1 g: Divide both sides by 5: 1000 mg is in (1000 / 5) mL 1 g is in 200 mL.
    Solution: 1 in 200
  4. What is 0.2% w/v expressed as 1 in X?
    0.2% w/v means 0.2 grams in 100 mL. We want to find out how many millilitres contain 1 gram. If 0.2 g is in 100 mL, To get to 1 g, we need to multiply 0.2 g by (1 / 0.2) = 5. So, multiply the volume by 5: 100 mL × 5 = 500 mL. Thus, 1 gram is in 500 mL.
    Solution: 1 in 500
  5. If a mixture is described as 15mg/ml, how many grams are required to make 80 millilitres of the mixture?
    First, find total milligrams: 15 mg/mL × 80 mL = 1200 mg Convert milligrams to grams: 1200 mg ÷ 1000 mg/g = 1.2 g
    Solution: 1.2 g
  6. If a drug is described as 1 part in 250, how many litres are required to provide a dose of 50g?
    "1 part in 250" means 1 gram in 250 mL. We need 50 grams. This is 50 times the amount. So, 50 g will be in 50 × 250 mL = 12500 mL. Convert millilitres to litres: 12500 mL ÷ 1000 mL/L = 12.5 L
    Solution: 12.5 L
  7. How many millilitres of a 2.5%w/v solution would be required to provide a dose of 100mg?
    Convert %w/v to mg/mL: 2.5% w/v = 2.5 grams in 100 mL = 2500 mg in 100 mL. So, 2500 mg ÷ 100 mL = 25 mg/mL. Now, find the volume needed for 100 mg: Volume = Total dose ÷ Concentration Volume = 100 mg ÷ 25 mg/mL = 4 mL
    Solution: 4 ml
  8. How much Calcium Carbonate in grams is required to make 150 millimoles, given that the molecular weight of Calcium Carbonate = 100?
    1 mole of Calcium Carbonate weighs 100 grams. 1000 millimoles = 100 grams. We need 150 millimoles. 150 millimoles is 150/1000 = 0.15 moles. So, 0.15 × 100 g = 15 g
    Solution: 15 g
  9. How many grams of Potassium Permanganate (molecular weight = 158) are required to produce 500 millilitres of a 0.5 molar solution?
    1 molar (1M) solution means 158 g in 1000 mL. 0.5 molar (0.5M) solution means 0.5 × 158 g in 1000 mL = 79 g in 1000 mL. We need to make 500 mL, which is half of 1000 mL. So, we need half of 79 g: 79 g ÷ 2 = 39.5 g
    Solution: 39.5 g
  10. You need to make 200mL of a 6M solution from a 20M stock solution, how many millilitres of stock solution do you require?
    Use the formula C1V1 = C2V2: (20 M) × V1 = (6 M) × (200 mL) 20 × V1 = 1200 V1 = 1200 ÷ 20 V1 = 60 mL
    Solution: 60 ml
  11. If a person is 5 feet and 1 inch tall, what is this in meters (Assuming 1 inch = 2.5 cm)?
    Convert feet to inches: 5 feet × 12 inches/foot = 60 inches. Total height in inches = 60 inches + 1 inch = 61 inches. Convert inches to centimetres: 61 inches × 2.5 cm/inch = 152.5 cm. (Calculation: 61 × 2 = 122; 61 × 0.5 = 30.5; 122 + 30.5 = 152.5) Convert centimetres to metres: 152.5 cm ÷ 100 cm/m = 1.525 meters
    Solution: 1.525 meters
  12. If a patient is 10 stone 10 lbs, how much do they weigh in Kg (Assuming 2 lbs = 1 Kg)?
    Convert stone to pounds: 10 stone × 14 lbs/stone = 140 lbs. Total weight in pounds = 140 lbs + 10 lbs = 150 lbs. Convert pounds to kilograms: 150 lbs ÷ 2 lbs/Kg = 75 Kg
    Solution: 75 Kg
  13. What is the body surface area of someone who is 1.8 metres tall and 80 Kg in weight?
    (For Your Information: √3.61 = 1.9, √4.00 = 2.0, √4.41 = 2.1, √4.84 = 2.2)
    Formula: BSA = √(Ht (cm) x Weight (kg) / 3600)
    Convert height to cm: 1.8 metres = 180 cm. BSA = √((180 cm × 80 Kg) ÷ 3600) Simplify the fraction first: (180 × 80) ÷ 3600 (18 × 80) ÷ 360 (cancel a zero) (18 × 8) ÷ 36 144 ÷ 36 = 4 BSA = √4.00 From the given list, √4.00 = 2.0.
    Solution: 2.0 m²
  14. What is 50 °F in °C?
    Use the formula C = (F - 32) × 5/9: C = (50 - 32) × 5/9 C = 18 × 5/9 Simplify the multiplication: (18 ÷ 9) × 5 = 2 × 5 = 10. C = 10 °C
    Solution: 10 °C
  15. How many millilitres of a 1 in 200 solution is required to produce 1000mL of a 1 in 50,000 solution?
    Use the formula C1V1 = C2V2. C1 = 1/200 V1 = ? C2 = 1/50000 V2 = 1000 mL (1/200) × V1 = (1/50000) × 1000 To isolate V1, multiply both sides by the reciprocal of C1 (which is 200/1): V1 = (1/50000) × 1000 × (200/1) V1 = (1000 × 200) / 50000 V1 = 200000 / 50000 V1 = 200 / 50 (cancel zeros) V1 = 4 mL
    Solution: 4 ml
  16. If a 75-year-old male has an ideal body weight of 60kg and an actual body weight of 70kg with a serum creatinine of 140 micromol/litre, what is their CrCl?
    Formula: CrCl (ml/min) = (140-Age) x Weight (Kg) / Serum creatinine (micromol/L)
    CrCl (ml/min) = (140 - 75) × 60 ÷ 140 CrCl (ml/min) = 65 × 60 ÷ 140 CrCl (ml/min) = 3900 ÷ 140 CrCl (ml/min) = 390 ÷ 14 CrCl (ml/min) = 27.85... Rounding to one decimal place for consistency with previous answers:
    Solution: 27.9 ml/min
  17. If Zinc Oxide cream is described as 10% w/w, in milligrams, how much active ingredient will be required to make 250 grams?
    10% w/w means 10 grams of active ingredient in 100 grams of cream. We need to make 250 grams of cream. If 10g is in 100g, then for 250g: (10g / 100g) × 250g = (1/10) × 250 g = 25 g. Convert grams to milligrams: 25 g × 1000 mg/g = 25,000 mg
    Solution: 25,000 mg
  18. A 50 Kg patient requires 12 mg/Kg/minute for 5 minutes. This is to be delivered from a 6% w/v solution, how much solution will they need in ml?
    First, calculate the total amount of drug needed: 12 mg/Kg/minute × 50 Kg × 5 minutes = 3000 mg. Next, understand the concentration of the stock solution: 6% w/v = 6 grams in 100 mL = 6000 mg in 100 mL. Now, find the volume of solution needed: Volume = (Total drug needed / Concentration in mg) × Volume for concentration Volume = (3000 mg / 6000 mg) × 100 mL Simplify the fraction 3000/6000: 3000/6000 = 3/6 = 1/2 Volume = (1/2) × 100 mL Volume = 50 mL
    Solution: 50 ml
  19. If a patient requires 25 mcg/minute of a drug for 2 hours, what is the total amount of drug they will receive in mg? Answer to 1 decimal place.
    First, convert hours to minutes: 2 hours × 60 minutes/hour = 120 minutes. Calculate total micrograms: 25 mcg/minute × 120 minutes = 3000 mcg. Convert micrograms to milligrams: 3000 mcg ÷ 1000 mcg/mg = 3.0 mg.
    Solution: 3.0 mg
  20. If the formula for Oral Electrolyte Solution is:
    • Sodium Chloride: 260mg
    • Potassium Chloride: 150mg
    • Glucose: 2000mg
    • Purified Water: to 100 mL
    How much Sodium Chloride and Potassium Chloride in milligrams are required to produce 500ml of the Oral Electrolyte Solution?
    The formula is for a total volume of 100 mL. We need to produce 500 mL. Calculate the scaling factor: 500 mL ÷ 100 mL = 5. Multiply the Sodium Chloride quantity by the scaling factor: Sodium Chloride needed = 260 mg × 5 = 1,300 mg Multiply the Potassium Chloride quantity by the scaling factor: Potassium Chloride needed = 150 mg × 5 = 750 mg
    Solution: Sodium Chloride: 1,300 mg, Potassium Chloride: 750 mg
