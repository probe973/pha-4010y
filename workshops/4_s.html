Imperial and Metric Units for Volumes

For this section you should use:

  • 1 pint $\approx$ 500 ml
  • 1 gallon = 8 pints
  1. Convert the following volumes into ml:

    a. 3 pints: $3~\text{pints} \times 500~\text{ml}/\text{pint} = 1500~\text{ml}$

    b. 0.5 pints: $0.5~\text{pints} \times 500~\text{ml}/\text{pint} = 250~\text{ml}$

    c. 3 gallons: $3~\text{gallons} \times 8~\text{pints}/\text{gallon} \times 500~\text{ml}/\text{pint} = 12000~\text{ml}$



  2. Convert the following volumes into pints:

    a. 2000 ml: $2000~\text{ml} / 500~\text{ml}/\text{pint} = 4~\text{pints}$

    b. 3.5 l: $3.5~\text{l} = 3500~\text{ml}$, so $3500~\text{ml} / 500~\text{ml}/\text{pint} = 7~\text{pints}$

    c. 4 gallons: $4~\text{gallons} \times 8~\text{pints}/\text{gallon} = 32~\text{pints}$

    d. 125 ml: $125~\text{ml} / 500~\text{ml}/\text{pint} = 0.25~\text{pints}$



Imperial and Metric Units for Mass

For this section you should use:

  • 1 $\text{kg}\approx2$ lbs (pounds)
  • 1 $\text{lb}=16~\text{oz}$ (ounces)
  • 1 stone = 14 lbs
  1. Convert the following masses into lbs (pounds):

    a. 5 kg: $5~\text{kg} \times 2~\text{lbs}/\text{kg} = 10~\text{lbs}$

    b. 500 g: $500~\text{g} = 0.5~\text{kg}$, so $0.5~\text{kg} \times 2~\text{lbs}/\text{kg} = 1~\text{lb}$

    c. 3500 g: $3500~\text{g} = 3.5~\text{kg}$, so $3.5~\text{kg} \times 2~\text{lbs}/\text{kg} = 7~\text{lbs}$

    d. 32 oz: $32~\text{oz} / 16~\text{oz}/\text{lb} = 2~\text{lbs}$

    e. 8 oz: $8~\text{oz} / 16~\text{oz}/\text{lb} = 0.5~\text{lbs}$

    f. 2 stone: $2~\text{stone} \times 14~\text{lbs}/\text{stone} = 28~\text{lbs}$

    g. 10 stone 4 lbs: $(10~\text{stone} \times 14~\text{lbs}/\text{stone}) + 4~\text{lbs} = 140~\text{lbs} + 4~\text{lbs} = 144~\text{lbs}$

    h. 12 stone 12 lbs: $(12~\text{stone} \times 14~\text{lbs}/\text{stone}) + 12~\text{lbs} = 168~\text{lbs} + 12~\text{lbs} = 180~\text{lbs}$



  2. Convert the following into stone:

    a. 280 lbs: $280~\text{lbs} / 14~\text{lbs}/\text{stone} = 20~\text{stone}$

    b. 84 lbs: $84~\text{lbs} / 14~\text{lbs}/\text{stone} = 6~\text{stone}$

    c. 70 kg: $70~\text{kg} \times 2~\text{lbs}/\text{kg} = 140~\text{lbs}$, so $140~\text{lbs} / 14~\text{lbs}/\text{stone} = 10~\text{stone}$



  3. Convert the following masses into stone and lbs (e.g. 12 stone 5 lbs):

    a. 150 lbs: $150~\text{lbs} / 14 = 10$ with remainder $10~\text{lbs}$, so 10 stone 10 lbs

    b. 200 lbs: $200~\text{lbs} / 14 = 14$ with remainder $4~\text{lbs}$, so 14 stone 4 lbs

    c. 55 kg: $55~\text{kg} \times 2~\text{lbs}/\text{kg} = 110~\text{lbs}$, so $110~\text{lbs} / 14 = 7$ with remainder $12~\text{lbs}$, so 7 stone 12 lbs

    d. 82 kg: $82~\text{kg} \times 2~\text{lbs}/\text{kg} = 164~\text{lbs}$, so $164~\text{lbs} / 14 = 11$ with remainder $10~\text{lbs}$, so 11 stone 10 lbs



Imperial and Metric Units for Length

For this section you should use:

  • 1 inch $\approx$ 2.5 cm
  • 1 ft (foot) = 12 inches
  1. Convert the following lengths into cm:

    a. 10 inches: $10~\text{inches} \times 2.5~\text{cm}/\text{inch} = 25~\text{cm}$

    b. 1 ft: $1~\text{ft} = 12~\text{inches}$, so $12~\text{inches} \times 2.5~\text{cm}/\text{inch} = 30~\text{cm}$

    c. 5 ft 3 inches: $5~\text{ft} = 60~\text{inches}$, so $(60+3)~\text{inches} \times 2.5~\text{cm}/\text{inch} = 63~\text{inches} \times 2.5~\text{cm}/\text{inch} = 157.5~\text{cm}$

    d. 6 ft 6 inches: $6~\text{ft} = 72~\text{inches}$, so $(72+6)~\text{inches} \times 2.5~\text{cm}/\text{inch} = 78~\text{inches} \times 2.5~\text{cm}/\text{inch} = 195~\text{cm}$



  2. Convert the following lengths into ft and inches (e.g. 5 ft 8 inches):

    a. 240 inches: $240~\text{inches} / 12~\text{inches}/\text{ft} = 20~\text{ft}$

    b. 100 inches: $100~\text{inches} / 12~\text{inches}/\text{ft} = 8$ with remainder $4~\text{inches}$, so 8 ft 4 inches

    c. 75 inches: $75~\text{inches} / 12~\text{inches}/\text{ft} = 6$ with remainder $3~\text{inches}$, so 6 ft 3 inches

    d. 50 cm: $50~\text{cm} / 2.5~\text{cm}/\text{inch} = 20~\text{inches}$, so $20~\text{inches} / 12~\text{inches}/\text{ft} = 1$ with remainder $8~\text{inches}$, so 1 ft 8 inches

    e. 2 m: $2~\text{m} = 200~\text{cm}$, so $200~\text{cm} / 2.5~\text{cm}/\text{inch} = 80~\text{inches}$, so $80~\text{inches} / 12~\text{inches}/\text{ft} = 6$ with remainder $8~\text{inches}$, so 6 ft 8 inches



Body Mass Index

The formula for body mass index (BMI):

$BMI=\frac{\text{Mass}(\text{kg})}{(\text{Height}(\text{m}))^{2}}$

  1. What are the units of the BMI measurement?

    Units are $\text{kg}/\text{m}^{2}$



  2. Find the BMI of the following people:

    a. Mass=90 $\text{kg}$, Height=2 $\text{m}$: $BMI = \frac{90~\text{kg}}{(2~\text{m})^{2}} = \frac{90}{4}~\text{kg}/\text{m}^{2} = 22.5~\text{kg}/\text{m}^{2}$

    b. Mass=64 $\text{kg}$, Height=1.6 $\text{m}$: $BMI = \frac{64~\text{kg}}{(1.6~\text{m})^{2}} = \frac{64}{2.56}~\text{kg}/\text{m}^{2} = 25~\text{kg}/\text{m}^{2}$

    c. Mass=64.8 $\text{kg}$, Height=180 $\text{cm}$: $180~\text{cm} = 1.8~\text{m}$, so $BMI = \frac{64.8~\text{kg}}{(1.8~\text{m})^{2}} = \frac{64.8}{3.24}~\text{kg}/\text{m}^{2} = 20~\text{kg}/\text{m}^{2}$



Body Surface Area

The formula for body surface area (BSA):

$BSA(\text{m}^{2})=\sqrt{\frac{\text{Height}(\text{cm})\times \text{Mass}(\text{Kg})}{3600}}$

The following information may be useful:

$\sqrt{2}\approx1.41\quad\sqrt{3}\approx1.73\quad\sqrt{5}\approx2.24\quad\sqrt{6}\approx2.45$

  1. Find the BSA of the following people:

    a. Height=180 $\text{cm}$, Mass=60 $\text{kg}$: $BSA = \sqrt{\frac{180 \times 60}{3600}} = \sqrt{\frac{18 \times 6}{36}} = \sqrt{\frac{108}{36}} = \sqrt{3} \approx 1.73~\text{m}^{2}$

    b. Height=144 $\text{cm}$, Mass=50 $\text{kg}$: $BSA = \sqrt{\frac{144 \times 50}{3600}} = \sqrt{\frac{144 \times 1}{72}} = \sqrt{2} \approx 1.41~\text{m}^{2}$

    c. Height=1.92 $\text{m}$, Mass=112.5 $\text{kg}$: $1.92~\text{m} = 192~\text{cm}$, so $BSA = \sqrt{\frac{192 \times 112.5}{3600}} = \sqrt{\frac{4 \times 112.5}{75}} = \sqrt{\frac{450}{75}} = \sqrt{6} \approx 2.45~\text{m}^{2}$



Ideal Body Weight (IBW)

Use this formula for IBW:

Female: IBW ($\text{kg}$) = Height ($\text{cm}$)$\times0.9-92$

Male: IBW ($\text{kg}$) = Height ($\text{cm}$)$\times0.9-88$

  1. What is the IBW for the following:

    a. Male, Height = 1.7 m: $1.7~\text{m} = 170~\text{cm}$, so $IBW = 170~\text{cm} \times 0.9 - 88 = 153 - 88 = 65~\text{kg}$

    b. Female, Height = 1.8 m: $1.8~\text{m} = 180~\text{cm}$, so $IBW = 180~\text{cm} \times 0.9 - 92 = 162 - 92 = 70~\text{kg}$



Mixed Question

  1. For a person who is:

    Male; Height $=6$ ft; Mass $=14$ stone 4 lbs

    Find, using the conversions given in the earlier part of this worksheet:

    a. Their BSA

    Height in cm: $6~\text{ft} = 72~\text{inches}$, $72~\text{inches} \times 2.5~\text{cm}/\text{inch} = 180~\text{cm}$

    Mass in kg: $14~\text{stone}~4~\text{lbs} = (14 \times 14) + 4 = 196 + 4 = 200~\text{lbs}$, $200~\text{lbs} / 2~\text{lbs}/\text{kg} = 100~\text{kg}$

    $BSA = \sqrt{\frac{180~\text{cm} \times 100~\text{kg}}{3600}} = \sqrt{\frac{180 \times 1}{36}} = \sqrt{5} \approx 2.24~\text{m}^{2}$

    b. Their IBW

    Height in cm: $6~\text{ft} = 180~\text{cm}$ (from part a)

    Male IBW = Height ($\text{cm}$)$\times0.9-88 = 180~\text{cm} \times 0.9 - 88 = 162 - 88 = 74~\text{kg}$
