Transposing equations

  1. Make x the subject of each of the following:

    a. $5x=b$

    $\frac{5x}{5}=\frac{b}{5}\rightarrow x=\frac{b}{5}$



    b. $z=a+x$

    $z-a=a+x-a$

    $z-a=x \rightarrow x=z-a$



    c. $y=mx+c$

    $y-c=mx+c-c$

    $y-c=mx$

    $\frac{y-c}{m}=\frac{mx}{m}$

    $x=\frac{y-c}{m}$



    d. $y-10=3(x-2)$

    $y-10=3x-6$

    $y-10+6=3x$

    $\frac{y-4}{3}=x$



    e. $\frac{x+5}{p}=q$

    $x+5=pq$

    $x=pq-5$



  2. In each of the following, make the given variable [in brackets] the subject:

    a. $C_{1}V_{1}=C_{2}V_{2}$ [V1]

    $\frac{C_{1}V_{1}}{C_{1}}=\frac{C_{2}V_{2}}{C_{1}}$

    $V_{1}=\frac{C_{2}V_{2}}{C_{1}}$



    b. $E=h\frac{c}{\lambda}$ [h]

    $E\lambda=hc$

    $\frac{E\lambda}{c}=h$



    c. $I=\frac{m}{h^{2}}$ [h]

    $Ih^{2}=m$

    $h^{2}=\frac{m}{I}$

    $h=\sqrt{\frac{m}{I}}$



    d. $A=\frac{\sqrt{HM}}{60}$ [M]

    $60A=\sqrt{HM}$

    $(60A)^{2}=HM$

    $\frac{3600A^{2}}{H}=M$



Mixed Questions

  1. The body mass index (BMI) of a person is calculated as:

    $BMI=\frac{Mass(Kg)}{(Height(m))^{2}}$

    Find the height of a person in centimetres, given that they have a body mass index of 22.5 and a mass of 90000 g.

    $22.5=\frac{90}{h^{2}}$

    $22.5h^{2}=90$

    $h^{2}=\frac{90}{22.5}$

    $h=\sqrt{4}=2~m=200~cm$



  2. The wavelength of a light wave ($\lambda$), in m, can be calculated using the speed of light (c) and the frequency (v):

    $\lambda=\frac{c}{v}$

    It is found that a light wave has a wavelength of $1.5\times10^{-5}mm$. The speed of light is $3\times10^{8}ms^{-1}$

    a. Convert the wavelength into metres

    $1.5\times10^{-5}\times10^{-3}=1.5\times10^{-8}m$

    b. By rearranging the equation, and using your answer to part a, find the frequency of the light wave.

    $\lambda v=c \rightarrow v=\frac{c}{\lambda}$

    $v=\frac{3\times10^{8}ms^{-1}}{1.5\times10^{-8}m}$

    $v=\frac{3}{1.5}\times\frac{10^{8}}{10^{-8}}s^{-1}=2\times10^{16}s^{-1}$
